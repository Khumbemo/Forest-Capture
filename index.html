Quick Start Guide
🚀 Getting Started ▶
Step 1: Go to Tools tab (bottom navigation).
Step 2: Tap + New to create a survey session. Give it a name (e.g., "Plot A — Sal Forest"), location, and investigator name.
Step 3: Your new survey becomes the Active Survey. All data you record will be linked to it.
Step 4: Tap any of the 9 tool cards to start collecting data.

💡 Tip: You can switch between surveys anytime using the dropdown at the top of the Tools screen.
🗺️ Map & GPS ▶
• View your live position on an interactive map with satellite, terrain, and hybrid layers.
• Tap ⊕ Locate Me to center on your GPS position.
• Drop waypoints — name them and they're saved to your survey.
• GPS data includes coordinates, accuracy (±m), altitude, and UTM format.
• Offline tiles: Download map tiles for your area before going to the field.
📐 Quadrat Sampling ▶
• Set quadrat size (m²), number, and shape.
• Add species entries with: scientific name, count, DBH (cm), height (m), growth stage, and phenology.
• Species autocomplete draws from a built-in database + your survey history.
• Each quadrat is saved to a table — tap to edit or delete entries.

💡 Tip: The Analytics module calculates diversity indices from quadrat data.
📏 Belt Transect ▶
• Define transect length, width, and bearing (0–360°).
• Record start/end GPS with one tap.
• Add intercept entries — species, distance, and canopy cover %.
• Multiple transects can be saved per survey.
🌡️ Environment ▶
• Auto-Fill pulls live temperature, humidity, and altitude.
• Record slope, aspect, elevation, and canopy cover %.
• Canopy estimation: Take an upward photo for green fraction proxy.
• Log soil type, moisture level, and soil color.
🔥 Disturbance & CBI ▶
Disturbance: Toggle grazing, logging, fire, human activity with severity (1–5).

CBI: Rate burn severity across 5 strata. Composite score (0–3) auto-calculated.
0 = Unburned → 3 = High severity burn.
📸 Media ▶
Photos: Auto-compressed and uploaded to cloud. Link to quadrat numbers.
Voice Notes: Record audio observations in the field.
Field Notes: Categorized text notes with optional GPS auto-fill.
📊 Analytics ▶
Auto-computed from quadrat data:
• Shannon-Wiener H′ — diversity index
• Simpson's Index — dominance measure
• Pielou's J′ — evenness
• IVI — Importance Value Index
• DBH Histogram — diameter distribution

Compare Surveys: Side-by-side biodiversity comparison.
📤 Export & Backup ▶
Export as CSV, JSON, GPX, or HTML report.
Backup: Export entire database. Restore anytime — data is preserved.
🌿 E-Herbarium ▶
Create digital herbarium vouchers with full taxonomy, GPS, ecology data, and specimen photos.
Export as .doc — formatted label ready for printing.
📶 Offline & Sync ▶
• Offline-first: All data saved locally. No internet needed.
• Data syncs to Firebase when online.
• Login once — session persists offline.
• Pre-download map tiles for remote areas.

💡 Tip: Back up data before remote field sites.
